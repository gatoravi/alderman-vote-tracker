Resolution No. 35

June Helbling

WHEREAS, June Helbling was born at
home in St. Louis, Missouri on June 1, 1913
to William and Ann Murphy Bleines. June
has a fraternal twin, May and 3 older sisters,
Teresa, Margaret and Loretta and a younger
sister, Ceil, making a total of 6 girls; and

Mary and Kerry and five great grandchildren;
Drew, Luke, June, Liam, and Zach and 3 more
expected in 2013 making a total of 8; and

WHEREAS, June worked at St. Louis
University Medical School for six years for
the  Nobel  award-winning  Dr.  Edward
Adelbert Doisy. She also worked for as a legal
secretary for 12 years and after her children
were in school she worked 10 years for a dental
office in south St. Louis; and

WHEREAS, June learned to swim as an
adult at the same time her children were taking
swimming lesson. Her children were so proud
to have a Mom who was a Flying Shark!
She also loved to sew and made many dresses
for her daughters and her nieces. June’s other
great and lifelong interest has been Bridge. She
began to play Bridge at the age of 16 which
means  she  has  been  playing  for  84  years.
Although she did not acquire points toward a
Life Master designation, she is well respected
by her bridge-playing friends including Life
Masters.

NOW  THEREFORE  BE 

IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to congratulate June Helbling on
the occasion of her 100th Birthday and we
wish her continued peace, good health and
happiness and we further direct the Clerk of
this Board to spread a copy of this Resolution
across the minutes of these proceedings and
to prepare a commemorative copy to the end
that it may be presented to our honoree at a
time  and  place  deemed  appropriate  by  the
Sponsor.

Resolution No. 37

Delta Day at St. Louis City Hall
WHEREAS,  Delta  Sigma  Theta
Sorority,  Inc.  was  founded  on  January  13,
1913,  by  twenty-two  visionary  woman  at
Howard University; and

WHEREAS, since that day for over 100
years  Delta  members  have  been  actively
involved  in  almost  every  major  social
movement in the United States; and

WHEREAS,  the  first  public  act
performed  by  the  Delta  founders  involved
their  participation  in  the  historic Women’s
Suffrage March in Washington, D.C. during
March of 1913; and

WHEREAS,  originally  chartered  as
Lambda  Sigma,  the  Saint  Louis Alumnae
Chapter  of  Delta  Sigma  Theta  has  been
committed to public service for over 90 years;
and

WHEREAS,  every  Delta  uses  her
influence for the enactment of laws to protect
the unfortunate and for the repeal of those
laws which deprive human beings of their
privileges and rights; and

WHEREAS,  Delta  Sigma  Theta  is
increasingly involved in important issues at
the local, state and federal levels in global
issues,  and  recently  received  special
consultative status with the United Nations;
and

WHEREAS,  Deltas  from  across  the
nation  travel  to  Washington,  D.C.  to
participate  in  “Delta  Days  in  the  Nation’s
Capitol”; and

WHEREAS, on Friday, May 10, 2013,
Deltas  from  throughout  the  St.  Louis
metropolitan area will gather to celebrate the
annual “Delta Day at St. Louis City Hall.”

NOW  THEREFORE  BE 

IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our

this  Board 

deliberations  to  recognize  and  honor  the
members of the St. Louis Alumnae Chapter of
Delta Sigma Theta Sorority and we thank them
for  their  tireless  efforts  on  behalf  of  the
citizens of this community and we direct the
Clerk  of 
to  prepare  a
commemorative copy of this Resolution to
the  end  that  it  may  be  presented  to  our
honorees  at  a  time  and  place  deemed
appropriate by the Sponsor.

Eagle Scout Robert A. St. Pierre

on Saturday, May 11, 2013 the rank of Eagle
Scout will be awarded to Robert A. St. Pierre;
and

WHEREAS, those who are familiar with
Scouting are aware that the rank of Eagle Scout
is a goal sought by many but attained by few;
requiring  intelligence,  perseverance,  and  a
strong sense of service to your community;
and

WHEREAS, Robert A. St. Pierre is a
member  of  Boy  Scout  Troop  181,
Thunderbird District, Greater St. Louis Area
Council; and

WHEREAS, Robert joined Greater St.
Louis Area Council Boy Scout Troop 181 on
February 5, 2006. He received his Scout badge
on March 2, 2006 and his Tenderfoot badge
on September 7, 2006.  He attained his Second
Class  badge  on  December  9,  2006,  and
received his First Class Badge on February
10, 2007.  He was awarded his Star badge on
July 7, 2007, and his Life badge on March 6,
2008; and

WHEREAS,  along  the  path  to  Eagle
Scout, Robert spent 157 nights camping, 107
miles hiking, and performed 145 service hours.
Over the years, he served as Troop Guide,
Scribe, Assistant Patrol Leader, Quartermaster
for 3 years, and Junior Assistant Scoutmaster.
He  is  currently  registered  as  an Assistant
Scoutmaster; and

WHEREAS, in 2010, Robert completed
his Eagle Project.  He built five picnic tables
for  the  Carondelet  Park  Rec  Complex
(YMCA),  for  the  Y’s  Summer  Camp
programs; and

WHEREAS, in 2011, after volunteering
at least 50 hours for the YMCA Book Fair,
Robert received the Responsibility Award, in
recognition of doing the right thing and being
honestly accountable for actions and attitude;
and

WHEREAS, Robert earned the rank of
Eagle  Scout  on  December  20,  2012,  after
completing 28 merit badges (21 of which are
required for the Eagle rank); and

WHEREAS, he is a senior at McKinley
Classical  Leadership Academy,  and  will
graduate  in  May  2013  and  will  attend
Missouri  S  &  T  (University  of  Missouri
Science and Technology) beginning this Fall,
and plans to study Computer Engineering.

IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, being apprised of the
