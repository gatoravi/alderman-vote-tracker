Resolution No. 231
Ms. Ruby Perryman

WHEREAS, Ruby Perryman was born
December 13, 1928.  She was the youngest
daughter of seven children born to Amos and
Eva Bell Perryman; and

WHEREAS,  Ruby’s  siblings  were
Goodney Perryman, Anna Mary Perryman-
Townsend,  Lonzell  Perryman,  Mattie
Perryman-McGlothan, Julia Mae Perryman-
Smith, Lee Perryman.  All have preceded her
in death; and

WHEREAS, Ruby was educated at the
elementary  and  secondary  schools  in
Sunflower, Mississippi; and

WHEREAS, Ruby met and married Less
Campbell after which the couple moved to St.
Louis, Missouri to raise their  ten children in
1952.  Ruby’s children are:  Less Campbell
Jr., Corine Campbell, James Campbell, John
Campbell,  David  Campbell,  Michael
Campbell, Mary Campbell, Eli Campbell,
Kevin Campbell and Redman Campbell.  All
transitioned before Ruby; and

WHEREAS, Ruby was a very dedicated
and caring nurse as she served her patients at
the Normandy Hospital where she worked
for over  thirty years before retiring; and

WHEREAS, Ruby was a very active
and involved leader in the Order of Eastern
Star, there she encouraged many new “Sisters
of the Order” while she strategically moved
through ranks to become Worthy Matron of
Electa Chapter No.1 in June of 1999.  There
she led that chapter with a deep consciousness
for the rules of the Order of Eastern Starr.
Ruby  was  an  excellent  member  of  Electa
Chapter No. 1 for over 35 years; and

WHEREAS, Ruby was a member of
Shalom Church City of Peace in Berkeley,
Missouri under the Pastorship of Reverend,
Dr. Freddie Clark where she worshiped until
her health began to decline.

NOW  THEREFORE  BE 

IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the  City  of  St.  Louis  that  we  pause  to
remember the many contributions of Ruby
Perryman to the citizens of the City of St.
Louis and we join with her many friends in
expressing our sorrow at her passing, and we
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes
of these proceedings and to prepare a memorial
copy for presentation to the Perryman family,
at a time and place deemed appropriate by
the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 21st day of February, 2014

by:
Honorable Chris Carter, Alderman 27th Ward

Resolution No. 232

Gitana Productions “Living the
Dream: 50 Years and Beyond”

WHEREAS, Gitana Productions was
launched in 1996 by Cecilia Nadal with the
objective of bringing to St. Louis a global vision
of music, dance and drama; and

WHEREAS,  Ms.  Nadal  and  partner
Danny  Clark  felt  that  many  St.  Louisans
lacked the opportunity to participate in arts
presentations which encouraged broad cross-
cultural awareness and appreciation and that
such experiences could heal and contribute to
progress in St. Louis; and

WHEREAS, students in Gitana’s Global
Education through the Arts program have, for
the last six years, toured greater St. Louis
through the months of February and March
delivering a message of peace and nonviolence
through a dynamic 45-minute performance
with music, drama and dance titled “Living
the Dream;” and

WHEREAS,  Gitana’s  programming
focuses  on  community  health  and  global
healing,  emphasizes  the  importance  of
compassion and love and the relationship
between  these  virtues  and  the  goals  of
diversity and nonviolence; and

WHEREAS,  this  years  “Living  the
Dream”  tour,  subtitiled  “50  Years  and
Beyond,” will feature youth who’ve been
trained in various forms of artistic expressions
who  will  entertain  and  educate  audiences
around the city, from the Juvenile Detention
Centers to the chambers of City Hall; and

WHEREAS, we commend the work of
Gitana and recognize the power of the arts in
transforming hearts and minds and healing
individuals, communities, cities and the world,
bridging divides, broadening understandings
of shared human experiences and encouraging
love while discouraging violence.

NOW  THEREFORE  BE 

IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize and thank Gitana
Productions  for  their  dedication  to  global
healing through music, dance and drama and
for their contributions to the City of St. Louis.
We further direct the Clerk of this Board to
spread a copy of this Resolution across the
minutes of these proceedings and to prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
presented to our honoree at a time and place

5.58.040 - Opening of bids.
A. Proposals shall be opened at the time
and place fixed by the advertisement, in the
presence  of  such  bidders  as  desire  to  be
present, and shall be open to the inspection
of bidders.

B.  The  bids  shall  not  be  materially
modified or amended as to price, specification
or otherwise, nor substitutions placed thereon,
after opening except when the lowest bid is
from a non-local bidder. When the lowest bid
is from a non-local bidder, any local bidder
within two percent of the lowest bid may
match the lowest bid. If a local bidder matches
the lowest bid, then the Supply Commissioner
may select the bid from the local bidder. If
more  than  one  local  bidder  is  within  two
percent of the lowest bid, then only the lowest
local bidder may match the bid. In all other
circumstances, modification, supplementation
or amendment shall cause rejection of the bid.
For purposes of this chapter, local bidder
means  a  bidder  whose  principal  place  of
business is within the City of St. Louis, has
had a valid business license for at least one
year, and is current in payment of local taxes.
Principal place of business shall be defined as
the business’s physical office, plant, or site
where  a  majority  (51%)  of  the  full-time
employees, chief officer, and managers of the
business regularly work and conduct business,
or where the plant or office and equipment
required for the furnishing of the goods or
performance of the services provided to the
City, as required by the contract, are physically
located in the City of St. Louis for at least one
taxable year immediately prior to the date of
the bid.
